Rental Information

Tenant Rights

Renting in Ontario: Key Steps and Tips

Steps:

  • Determine Budget & Area: Decide on your living costs and preferred neighborhood.
  • Find a Home: Look for a place that fits your criteria.
  • View the Property: Inspect the home in person.
  • Submit an Offer: Prepare your documents and make an offer.
  • Sign the Lease: Finalize the rental agreement.
  • Handle Finances: Secure tenant insurance, pay deposits, and future rent.
  • Move-In: Arrange the moving process, including elevator bookings for condos.

Tips:

  • Choose the Right Time: Aim to avoid peak rental seasons like September.
  • Prepare Documents: Have your ID, credit report, employment or student proof ready.
  • Understand Utility Responsibilities: Know which utilities you'll need to cover.
  • Lease Agreements: Opt for yearly contracts through agents for security and avoid risky sublets.

During Tenancy:

  • Rent Increases: Legal only after 12 months with advance notice, capped at 2.5% for 2023.
  • Continuation: Leases automatically turn month-to-month post-term.
  • Eviction Protections: Landlords must have a valid reason for eviction.
  • Eviction Protections: Landlords must have a valid reason for eviction.

Landlord Rights and Responsibilities

Landlord Responsibilities:

  • Tenant Selection: Landlords have the right to choose tenants based on factors like income, credit history, and rental background. Discrimination based on race, nationality, or religion is illegal.
  • Collecting Deposits: Landlords can collect a deposit not exceeding one month or one week's rent.
  • Collecting Rent: Landlords can collect full rent on its due date and may increase rent once every 12 months with a 90-day notice to tenants.
  • Property Maintenance: Landlords must maintain the property's cleanliness, safety, and provide basic amenities like heating, water, and electricity.
  • Entry to Premises: Landlords can enter the premises for maintenance, inspection, or in emergencies after providing at least 24 hours' written notice.
  • Eviction of Tenants: Landlords have the right to evict tenants for reasons specified in the Residential Tenancies Act, such as non-payment of rent or legal violations.

Landlord Rights:

  • Tenant Selection: Landlords have the right to choose suitable tenants without discrimination.
  • Collecting Deposits and Rent: Landlords have the right to collect deposits and rent payments.
  • Property Maintenance: Landlords have the right to maintain and repair the property to ensure its proper functioning.
  • Eviction of Tenants: Landlords have the right to evict tenants who violate regulations or cause problems, as per legal conditions.

Rental Contracts

Details on understanding and creating rental contracts. This section can include common terms, legal requirements, and tips for both landlords and tenants.

Firstly, did you know?

The Ontario government mandates that all rental contracts must use the standard lease template provided by the provincial government. This is to safeguard the rights of both tenants and landlords. The standard contract is clear and comprehensive, covering various possible scenarios in the leasing relationship, ensuring transparency and fairness in transactions for both parties.
